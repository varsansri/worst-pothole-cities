Potholes cost drivers billions in car damage, cause hundreds of fatalities, and signal deeper infrastructure failures. Here are the most thoroughly documented worst-pothole cities globally, with data on why they got this bad.

Top 10 Worst Cities Globally

1
Mumbai, India
Maharashtra, India
Monsoon season destroys patched roads every year. Poor-quality asphalt, high traffic density, and slow municipal repair create an annual cycle of deterioration. Over 2,000 potholes reported in a single monsoon season.
Severity: 9.8/10
2
Detroit, Michigan, USA
United States
Extreme freeze-thaw cycles, decades of municipal underfunding after the city's bankruptcy, and heavy commercial truck traffic. Average driver pays $1,800/year in pothole-related car repairs.
Severity: 9.5/10
3
Lagos, Nigeria
Nigeria, West Africa
Rapid urbanization has outpaced road infrastructure. High humidity + flooding + heavy vehicle overloading. Some roads haven't been repaved in 30+ years.
Severity: 9.3/10
4
Nairobi, Kenya
Kenya, East Africa
Heavy rain seasons, overloaded buses (matatus), and inadequate drainage create severe road damage in key corridors.
Severity: 9.0/10
5
New Orleans, Louisiana, USA
United States
Below sea-level city with perpetual subsidence. Roads crack as soil shifts. Hurricane damage repeatedly destroys repaired surfaces.
Severity: 8.8/10

Why Do Some Cities Have So Many Potholes?

  • Freeze-thaw cycles: Water seeps into cracks, freezes, expands, and breaks the asphalt from within. Northern US cities suffer heavily.
  • Monsoon flooding: Saturated soil beneath asphalt collapses. Mumbai's problem is largely this.
  • Underfunding: US infrastructure received a D+ from the American Society of Civil Engineers. Repair backlogs grow faster than budgets.
  • Low-quality materials: Political corruption in some regions leads to substandard road construction contracts.
  • Overloading: Commercial trucks over legal weight limits accelerate road degradation 10,000x faster than normal traffic.
Economic impact: The American Society of Civil Engineers estimates potholes cost US drivers $26 billion annually in vehicle damage. The UK loses £684 million/year. Globally, poor road quality costs an estimated $1.2 trillion in productivity, vehicle damage, and traffic delays.
