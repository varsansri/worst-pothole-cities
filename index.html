Worst Pothole Cities — Frequently Asked Questions

What city has the worst potholes in the world?

Mumbai ranks #1 with a damage score of 9.8/10. Over 10,000 potholes appear every monsoon season. Detroit ranks #2 at 9.4, where average annual repair costs hit $1,800 per driver — the highest in the USA.

What US city has the worst roads?

Detroit has the worst roads of any major US city, with 55,000+ potholes reported annually. Chicago, New Orleans ($1,500/yr), Kansas City, and Baltimore follow. NYC filled 298,000 potholes in 2023 alone.

How much do potholes cost car owners per year?

US drivers lose $600–$1,800/year on average to pothole damage. Damage includes tyre blowouts, bent rims, alignment, suspension components, and steering. Use our calculator above with your city and car type to get a personalised estimate.

What types of car damage do potholes cause?

Tyre punctures and blowouts, bent or cracked rims, wheel misalignment, damaged suspension struts and shocks, bent steering components, and undercarriage damage. Sports cars and luxury vehicles with low-profile tyres suffer the most.

Why does Mumbai have so many potholes?

Heavy monsoon rainfall infiltrates road surfaces, poor drainage, extreme traffic volume, maintenance budget shortfalls, and contractors using substandard materials. The city fills potholes continuously, but monsoon destroys them faster than crews can repair.

World's Worst Pothole Cities — Ranked by Road Damage Score

Potholes are more than an annoyance — they're a silent wealth drain for car owners worldwide. In Detroit alone, the average driver spends $1,800 per year on pothole-related car repairs. In Mumbai, over 10,000 potholes emerge every monsoon season, and the Bombay High Court has intervened multiple times over road-condition deaths. This ranking covers the 20 worst cities globally for road quality, with data on annual damage costs, pothole volume, and underlying causes.

The pothole damage calculator lets you estimate your personal annual cost based on your city, car type, and annual mileage. A luxury or sports car in Detroit can easily cost $3,000+ per year in pothole damage. A compact car in a lower-ranked city might cost $200. Your car type matters enormously — low-profile tyres and performance suspension are dramatically more vulnerable to pothole damage than standard factory configurations.

Climate plays a major role in road quality: freeze-thaw cycles (Chicago, Moscow) and monsoon flooding (Mumbai, Dhaka, Lagos) are the two primary pothole creation mechanisms. Cities in stable tropical or dry climates generally maintain better road surfaces for longer. Understanding which cities have the worst roads helps you anticipate and budget for vehicle maintenance.
