🕳️ How Much Are Potholes Costing Your Car?

A single pothole hit can cause thousands in damage — even at normal driving speeds. Here's a complete breakdown of what potholes do to your car, how much repairs cost, and how to claim compensation from local councils.

Damage by Car Component

🔧 Wheel Alignment
$100–$200
Most common pothole damage. Misalignment causes uneven tire wear and pulling. Get checked after any significant hit.
🛞 Tire Blowout / Sidewall Damage
$150–$350 per tire
Sharp pothole edges cut tire sidewalls. Run-flat tires (BMW, Mercedes) cost $300–$500+ each to replace.
💫 Bent or Cracked Wheel Rim
$200–$700+ per rim
Alloy rims are particularly vulnerable. Cosmetic repair (rim straightening) costs $100–$200. Structural damage = full replacement.
🔩 Suspension — Struts/Shocks
$300–$1,200
Repeated pothole impacts wear out struts and shocks prematurely. Symptoms: bouncy ride, clunking sounds, nose-dive under braking.
🔩 Control Arms & Ball Joints
$300–$1,000
Large potholes can bend or crack control arms. If a ball joint fails while driving, you can lose steering control entirely.
🛡️ Exhaust System Damage
$150–$600
Low-profile vehicles with limited ground clearance are especially vulnerable. Catalytic converters can crack on severe impacts.

How to Claim Pothole Compensation

  • Document everything: Photograph the pothole immediately (with a coin/object for scale), your damaged vehicle, and the road location/GPS
  • Get a repair estimate: Get 2–3 written quotes from mechanics
  • File with the local authority: In the US, file with your city/county road maintenance department. In the UK, file with the local council.
  • Use apps: FixMyStreet (UK), SeeClickFix (US) to officially report and track potholes
  • Small claims court: If denied, small claims court for amounts under $10,000 is an option
  • Insurance: Comprehensive auto insurance often covers pothole damage
Success rate: Pothole compensation claims have a 30–50% success rate if properly documented. Councils are more likely to pay if they had prior written notice of the pothole and failed to repair it within a reasonable timeframe.
